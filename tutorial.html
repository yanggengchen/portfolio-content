Basic Tutorial

Game Settings

Japanese Mahjong use 34 kind (37 if considering red tiles), 136 tiles in each game. Specifically:

  • 1-9 Man (abbr. 1m-9m)
  • 1-9 Pin (abbr. 1p-9p)
  • 1-9 Sou (abbr. 1s-9s)
  • 4 Winds: East, North, West, South (1z-4z)
  • 3 Dragon: White Green Red (5z-7z)
  • 3 Red Tiles: Red 5 Man, Red 5 Pin, Red 5 Sou (0m, 0p, 0s)

Rules

During each game, each player will draw 13 tiles as the start hand, then starting from the dealer, each player will draw a tile to the player's hand, and then discard a tile in his hand. Upon drawing the tile, if the 14 tiles in the player's hand satisfy a winning hand, the player wins the game.

How to play

We will first introduce some concepts in Japanese Mahjong:

  • Sequence: A set of 3 consecutive tiles (1m2m3m, 5p6p7p etc.)
  • Triplet: A set of 3 identical tiles (1m1m1m, 5z5z5z etc.)
  • Pair: A set of 2 identical tiles (1m1m etc.)

After a player has discarded a tile, other players can choose to execute one of the following calls:

  • Chii: If the tile is discarded by the previous player, and it can form a Sequence with 2 tiles in the player's hand, the player can use the 3 tiles to make a call
  • Pon: If the tile can form a Triplet with a pair in the player's hand, the player can use the 3 tiles to make a call
  • Kan: If the player has a Triplet identical to the discarded tile, the player can use the 4 tiles to make a call

After a player made a call, the player discard one tile and the game continues from the next counter-clockwise player. If the player Kanned, he first draw a tile from the bottom of the tile pile, then discard a tile.

Winning Hand

A Winning hand is only formed if the tiles in the player's hand or used to make a call by the player is one of the following cases:

  • 4 Sequence or Triplet + 1 Pair (1m2m3m7m7m7m4p5p6p2z2z2z5s5s)
  • 7 Pairs: (1m1m4m4m2p2p9p9p1s1s5z5z7z7z)
  • 13 Orphans: 1m9m1p9p1s9s1z2z3z4z5z6z7z + one arbitary tile in the previous 13 tiles

Upon satisfying one of the structure above, the tiles in the player's hand must also satisfy one of the Yaku requirements. If the last tile used to form a hand is obtained from another player, it must also not be in one of the following cases:

  • The player had discarded one of the potential winning tiles previously
  • A potential winning tile had been discarded in the same round and the player did not call it
  • A potential winning tile had been discarded after the player called Riichi, and the player did not call the tile
