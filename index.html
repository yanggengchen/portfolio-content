Introduction to Mahjong

Mahjong is a worldwide popular tile-based game created in China in the 19th century. Nowadays, it spread across Asia and many Western countries and had developed many regional variations. Most variations of Majong is played by four players, while some variations, for example, Japanese Mahjong, support three players playing Mahjong.

While most of the regional variations of Mahjong has differences in rules, winning hands, and some even has unique tiles, most variations of Mahjong share a set of basic rules, including the game setup, drawing tiles, discarding tiles, number of tile in a player's hand, actions available after another player discard a tile etc.

Currently, some popular regional variations of Mahjong includes: Competition Mahjong, Sichuan Mahjong, Japanese Mahjong etc.

Japanese Mahjong

Japanese Mahjong is a regional variation played in Japan. To win Japanese Mahjong, a player needs to have the most points among the four players after several games, where points are obtained through declaring winning hands in each game and calculated by the sum of number of Han(s) in a player's winning hand. Winning hand (or Hu) in Japanese Mahjong is restricted by Yaku and Furiten, meaning a player's hand must correspond to one of the special requirement, and a player must not win on a tile which other players discarded before in the same round, or previously discarded by the player himself. There are several other key differences between Japanese Mahjong and Competition Mahjong, except for Furiten, Japanese Mahjong does not have Flower tiles, have a set of special five tiles (five Man, five Pin, five Sou), 14 tiles among 136 tiles will not be drawn in a game, the dealer will continue to be the dealer if he wins a game.
