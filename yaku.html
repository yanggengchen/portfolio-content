Common Yakus

Riichi

You may declare Riichi when you had not used tiles from other players to make a call and you only need one more tile to declare a winning hand. After declaring Riichi, you must only discard the tile you draw and must not make calls.

Tips

Riichi is one of the most achieved Yakus and appears in over 40% of all winning hands. Therefore, if you do not need to make calls or your hand does not satisfy any other Yaku, Riichi is always a good choice. The drawback is that after declaring Riichi, you will not be able to do any defense, hence it is wise to not declare Riichi when defense is more important.

All Simples

All Simples is satisfied when all tiles in the hand does not contain Terminal Tiles (1m9m1p9p1s9s1z2z3z4z5z6z7z)

Tips

All Simples is another most achieved Yaku, appearing in nearly 20% of winning hands. Comparing to Riichi, it gives players ability to make calls using other player's tiles, which might significantly increase the speed of forming Sequences and Triplets.

Yakuhai

Yakuhai is the combination of three Yakus: Prevalent Wind, Seat Wind and Dragon Tiles. Prevalent Wind is containing the triplet of wind of the round, e.g. East Wind (1z) in East Round. Seat Wind is containing the triplet of wind of the seat, e.g. North Wind (4z) for the North Player. Dragon Tiles is one of the triplets of White, Green or Red tiles (5z, 6z, 7z)

Tips

Yakuhai is often a "Yaku saver". If you have a pair of Yakuhai at hand, you generally do not need to worry about not satisfying any Yakus. Often players with Yakuhai triplets will try to be the fastest player to reach Tenpai.
